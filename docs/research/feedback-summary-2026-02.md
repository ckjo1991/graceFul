# GraceFul Feedback Summary
Source: Volunteer Discussion (Chat)
Date: YYYY-MM-DD

---

# Overall Sentiment

Strongly supportive.

The idea was received positively and seen as helpful, especially for people who are not yet comfortable opening up in church settings.

---

# Key Insights

## 1. Barrier Reduction

- The platform lowers the barrier to sharing sensitive topics.
- Not everyone feels comfortable opening up publicly.
- Especially helpful for newcomers.
- Anonymous sharing increases psychological safety.

This directly validates the core concept.

---

## 2. Emotional Safety

Implicit assumption:
Some individuals want to share but lack the comfort or confidence to do so in person.

The platform provides:
- A gentler entry point
- A sense of being heard without exposure

---

## 3. Escalation Path Is Important

Suggestion:
Include an option for users to reach out directly.

Reasoning:
Knowing that “there are hands ready to help” increases trust and reassurance.

The presence of optional follow up enhances the perceived safety of the platform.

---

# Underlying Assumptions Identified

- Sharing is difficult for many people.
- New attendees are especially hesitant.
- Accessibility is more important than strict relational format.
- Anonymous support does not threaten in person prayer if positioned correctly.

---

# Risks Identified

No major objections raised.

Indirect design requirement:
The platform should clearly communicate that support beyond the app is available.

---

# Strategic Takeaway

This feedback validates the core hypothesis:

GraceFul solves a real friction problem.

Design implication:
Optional reach out functionality should be part of the MVP, not an afterthought.
